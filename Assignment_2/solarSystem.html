OUR SOLAR SYSTEM

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. It formed about 4.6 billion years ago when a dense region of a molecular cloud collapsed, forming the Sun and a protoplanetary disc.

The solar system consists of the Sun and its orbiting objects, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. The four inner rocky planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, while the four outer gas/ice giants are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Moons include Ganymede, the largest. Asteroids lie between Mars and Jupiter, while comets orbit the Sun.
